A day of feminist talks and workshops, aiming to bring different feminist discussions together

Saturday 1st November 2014 10am - 7pm

Wadham College, Parks Road, OX1 3PN

Admission free, donations welcome.

About

Feminism in Theory and Action is a day of talks and workshops aiming to bring together different feminist conversations. Our organisers are all active in various areas and have organised talks and workshops that reflect the multifaceted, consciously intersectional face of feminism today. We are keen to bridge the gap between student feminism and the daily struggles of women outside of universities.

Feminism in Theory and Action is intentionally political - we want to host a day of talks that focuses on what can be done to further feminist goals and to engage with everyday feminist struggles. The day will include panel discussions, workshops, documentary screenings, reading groups and more - there will even be an installation of feminist art!

We are still looking for people to help with the event. Please contact us if you are interested.

Speakers

Our speakers will include:

Art as Activism: Exploring the Portrayal of Colonised Bodies Panel:

  • Hana Riaz, writer, editor and director of The Body Narratives, and organiser of a major exhibition and series of workshops entitled 'A Different Mirror'
  • Indigo Williams, poet and cofounder of I Shape Beauty
  • Dr Ope Lori, a conceptual and political artist, who works primarily with the moving image and photography, around the politics of representation and identity, in popular culture and contemporary Fine Art practice
  • Sanaa Hamid, Visual Artist and Ambassador for Brown Girls
  • Shamsia Hassani, Iranian born street art and digital graffiti artist, based in Kabul (via video link)
  • Zarina Muhammad, artist and studying for a BA Fine Art at Central St Martins

Ecofeminism Panel:

  • Mary Mellor, Social Science Professor at Northumbria University
  • Ruthi Brandt, Green Party City Councillor for Carfax

Feminism and the Left Panel:

  • Nancy Lindisfarne, an Oxford-based academic and activist focusing on the relationship between gender and class

Feminism and the Legal System Panel:

  • Pragna Patel, Director of Southall Black Sisters, has been listed in The Guardian’s Top 100 women: activists and campaigners. SBS are a group of black and minority women who offer advice, advocacy and a resource centre in West London which provides a comprehensive service to women experiencing violence and abuse and other forms of inequality
  • Sarah Turnbull, a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Centre for Criminology (2013-2016). She responsible for the ‘Home and Away: Gender, Nation, Deportation’ project, which is part of a broader European Research Council funded project.Sarah also has an MA in Criminology and Sociolegal Studies (University of Toronto) and a BA in Women’s Studies (Simon Fraser University)
  • Wendy Petifer, from Haldane Society of Socialist Lawyers. Wendy has years of experience in legal aid, both law centres and private practice. She is currently a solicitor at Hackney Law Centre working with women migrants without documents.

First Responses to Sexual Assault: Queer-only Workshop:

  • Anna Bradshaw, OUSU Vice President for Women

Gendered Homelessness Panel:

  • Franki Hackett, spokesperson from Women's Aid, the key national charity working to end domestic violence against women and children
  • Sarah Hutchinson, speaking in her capacity as chair of Fabian Woman's Network. Sarah also works at the St Mungos Rebuilding Shattered Lives campaign and is a former Labour Councillor for Cowley
  • Focus E15 Mothers, a group of single mothers fighting for social housing not social cleansing in East London. Recently occupied Carpenter's estate in Stratford - their example has been making political waves across the country

Women and Mental Health Panel:

  • Bethany Lamont, founder and editor of Doll Hospital Journal, an art and literature magazine about women and mental health
  • Gladys Jusu-Sheriff, from WAND, Women's Association for African Networking and Development, a charity that helps (amongst other things) the mental health and wellbeing of immigrant African women in London boroughs
  • Dr Joan Rutherford, from the First-tier Tribunal, Health, Education and Social Care Chamber
  • Dr Steph Tolan MBBS, worked with refugee children suffering from mental health difficulties. She is a Foundation Year 2 Doctor at Torbay Hospital, South Devon Health Care Trust, currently working in Paediatric medicine. She trained at St George’s Hospital Medical School, London

Women and Class Panel:

  • Shanice McBean, ex-KCL intersectional feminist society president, anti-racist, anti-fascist activist
  • Ross Speer, socialist activist, Oxford DPhil Student
  • Speakers from Strike Magazine, a bimonthly publication dealing in politics, philosophy, art, subversion and sedition

Women and Seeking Asylum Panel:

  • ice&fire theatre group, performers who focus on spreading the word about human rights issues
  • Women for Refugee Women, a group who challenges the injustices experienced by women who seek asylum in the UK

Women and Work Panel:

  • Speakers from the Ritzy Workers Strike, a campaign against the Ritzy cinema group to pay its workers a living wage
  • Speakers from Sex Worker Open University, a collective of sex workers sharing skills and advice, while fighting their criminalisation
  • Speaker from Global Women's Strike, an International network for recognition and payment for all caring work
  • Ice and Fire
  • Women for Refugee Women
  • Lisa Muggeridge
  • Louise Livesey

Tickets

The event is free to attend, but we are crowdfunding to ensure that every person, of every gender, race, age and ability, is able to attend the conference without the prohibitively high costs of other feminism conferences. We are passionately committed to being able to cover our speakers' costs and ensure that the conference is free to attend. By crowd funding, we can hopefully raise the money we need through donations, and those who cannot spare much, or indeed anything, will still be able to come.
